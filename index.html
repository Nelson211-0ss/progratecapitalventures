The Challenge & Our Solution

In Uganda, over 80% of women-owned businesses operate in the informal sector, systematically excluded from traditional financing despite their proven business success.

The Challenge

Women entrepreneurs in Uganda's informal sector face significant barriers to accessing capital:

  • Traditional financial institutions require collateral and formal documentation
  • Limited property ownership rights make collateral-based lending impossible
  • Cultural biases restrict access to male-dominated financial networks
  • Lack of formal business registration prevents access to conventional investments
  • Existing microfinance solutions impose unsustainable interest rates and rigid repayment terms
Our Solution

Prograte Capital Ventures bridges this gap through our innovative approach:

  • Dividend-based micro-investments ranging from $15 to $500
  • No collateral requirements or formal documentation needed
  • Flexible payment schedules aligned with business cash flows
  • Comprehensive business support services including mentorship and training
  • Evaluation based on cash flow patterns, community reputation, and character

Our Investment Approach

A revolutionary model tailored for Uganda's informal sector

Dividend-Based Model

Unlike traditional loans with rigid repayment schedules, our dividend-based model aligns payments with business cash flows. Entrepreneurs make monthly dividend payments of 5-10% of invested capital, creating sustainable returns while accommodating the natural cycles of their businesses.

No Collateral Required

We've eliminated the collateral barrier that prevents women entrepreneurs from accessing traditional financing. Instead, we evaluate businesses through cash flow patterns, community reputation, and character assessments, creating opportunities for entrepreneurs who have been systematically excluded from the financial system.
